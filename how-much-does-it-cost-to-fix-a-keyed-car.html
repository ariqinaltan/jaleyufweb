How Much Does It Cost to Fix a Keyed Car?

 Posted on August 29, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  908 words  |  Billy Koelling

Having a keyed car is a frustrating and distressing experience for any car owner. It can significantly reduce the value and aesthetic appearance of your vehicle. Unfortunately, vandalism and intentional damage are common occurrences in our society, leading car owners to wonder how much it costs to fix a keyed car.

The cost of fixing a keyed car varies depending on several factors, such as the severity and extent of the damage, the type of car, and the location of the scratch. A minor scratch can be fixed with a simple touch-up paint job, while a deep scratch may require more extensive work, such as sanding and repainting.

Factors That Affect the Cost of Fixing a Keyed Car

There are several factors that can influence the cost of fixing a keyed car, such as:

The Extent of the Damage

The severity and length of the scratch determine the amount of work required. Deeper and longer scratches may require more in-depth and detailed repair work.

The Type of Car

The type of car you own can also affect the cost of repairs. Luxury cars often require specific paint and repair materials, resulting in higher repair costs.

The Location of the Scratch

The location of the scratch can also influence the repair cost. Scratches in visible and prominent areas of the car, such as the hood or doors, will require more meticulous repairs and can cost more.

The Cost of Fixing a Keyed Car

The cost of fixing a keyed car can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the severity of the damage. Here are some estimates for the cost of fixing a keyed car:

Touch-Up Paint Job

This method is suitable for minor scratches that don’t penetrate the clear coat of the vehicle. A touch-up paint job can cost between $50 and $300, depending on the quality of the products and the complexity of the job.

PDR (Paintless Dent Repair)

PDR is a less invasive method of repairing dents and scratches that don’t penetrate the clear coat. This method can cost between $100 and $500, depending on the extent of the damage.

Repainting the Area

If the scratch is extensive and penetrates the clear coat, the area will need to be sanded and repainted. Repainting can cost between $300 and $1500, depending on the extent of the damage and the type of car.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I fix a keyed car myself?

It is not recommended to fix a keyed car yourself, as it can lead to further damage and cost you more money in the long run. Professional auto body shops have the necessary tools and expertise to repair keyed cars accurately.

2. Should I file a police report if my car has been keyed?

Yes, you should file a police report if your car has been keyed. It can help with insurance claims and finding the person responsible for the damage.

3. Does insurance cover keyed cars?

Depending on your insurance policy, coverage for keyed cars can be included. Comprehensive insurance policies usually cover vandalism and intentional damage.

4. How long does it take to fix a keyed car?

The repair time for fixing a keyed car varies depending on the severity of the damage. Minor scratches can take a few hours, while deep scratches and repainting can take several days.

5. Can I still sell my car if it has been keyed?

Having a keyed car can reduce its value and aesthetic appearance. If you plan to sell your car, it’s recommended to have it repaired professionally beforehand.

6. Can I prevent my car from being keyed?

Although prevention is not always possible, there are some measures you can take to reduce the likelihood of having a keyed car, such as parking in well-lit and secure areas and investing in a car alarm system.

7. Is it possible to remove a large scratch from my car without repainting it?

If the scratch is not too deep and hasn’t penetrated the clear coat, it may be possible to remove it without repainting. However, it’s recommended to have a professional assess the damage.

8. How can I find a reliable auto body shop to fix my keyed car?

You can find a reliable auto body shop by asking for referrals from friends and family, checking online reviews, and verifying their licenses and certifications.

9. Does the repair of a keyed car affect its resale value?

Yes, having a keyed car can negatively impact its resale value. Therefore, it’s essential to have it repaired professionally to restore its appearance and value.

10. How long does a touch-up paint job last?

The durability of a touch-up paint job depends on several factors, such as the quality of the products used, the location of the scratch, and how well the job was done. Generally, touch-up paint jobs can last between 3 to 5 years.

11. Can I drive my car with a keyed scratch?

While driving with a keyed scratch doesn’t pose any safety risks, it can lead to more severe damage if not repaired promptly.

12. What is the difference between PDR and repainting?

PDR and repainting are both methods of repairing keyed cars, but they differ in the extent of the damage. PDR is suitable for dents and scratches that haven’t penetrated the clear coat, while repainting is required for deep and extensive scratches.
