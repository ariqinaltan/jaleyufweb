Howdy, partner! Hold Your Horses: Just How Long Do You Have to Press Assault Charges in Texas?

Been done wrong? Itchin' to see that varmint get what's comin' to them? Well, hold on to your Stetson there, 'cause we wouldn't want you runnin' off half-cocked. There's a time limit to consider, and depending on the severity of the sidewinder's crime, it might not be as quick a draw as you'd like.

The Statute of Limitations: Justice Ain't Always Speedy

Now, this here statute of limitations is basically a fancy way of sayin' there's a time limit to press charges. In Texas, it depends on whether you're dealin' with a misdemeanor assault or a felony assault:

  • Misdemeanor Assault: You've generally got yourself two years to hightail it down to the sheriff's office and file a report. That's right, partner, two whole years to bring that scoundrel to justice.

  • Felony Assault: Now, this here's a different story. There ain't no time limit for felony assault charges in Texas. So, you can take your sweet time plannin' your revenge...well, not exactly revenge, but definitely bringing them to justice.

The Takeaway: Know Your Rights and Giddy Up!

So, there you have it, pilgrim. The lowdown on how long you've got to press charges after an assault in Texas. Now, remember, this here ain't legal advice, and if you've been wronged, the best thing to do is mosey on down to a lawyer and get yourself squared away. But hey, at least you know the general timeframe for how long you've got to act.
