Meme Where Are They Now?

 Posted on August 17, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  978 words  |  Tandra Barner

Introduction to the World of Memes

Memes have become an integral part of internet culture, providing a means for people to communicate, entertain, and express themselves in a humorous and often relatable way. From the early days of the internet to the present, memes have evolved, and some have become iconic. But what happens to the subjects of these viral sensations after their fifteen minutes of fame? In this article, we delve into the “Meme Where Are They Now?” phenomenon, exploring the fates and fortunes of the individuals, animals, and even inanimate objects that have become meme legends.

The Rise of Memes

Before we explore where they are now, it’s important to understand how memes rise to fame. Memes can originate from various sources, such as social media, forums, or even accidental public moments captured on camera. They spread rapidly through sharing, adaptations, and iterations, often becoming a part of the internet’s collective consciousness.

Classic Memes and Their Protagonists

Some memes have stood the test of time, becoming classics in the meme hall of fame. These include the likes of “Bad Luck Brian,” “Success Kid,” and “Grumpy Cat,” among others. The individuals and pets behind these memes have experienced varying degrees of fame and impact on their personal lives.

Bad Luck Brian

“Bad Luck Brian” is a meme featuring a yearbook photo of a young man with an awkward smile and a plaid sweater vest. The meme captions describe various unfortunate, embarrassing, and hilarious scenarios.

Success Kid

“Success Kid” features a baby fist-pumping with a determined facial expression. The meme is used to celebrate small victories and successful outcomes.

Grumpy Cat

“Grumpy Cat,” whose real name was Tardar Sauce, became famous for her perpetually disgruntled expression, which was actually due to feline dwarfism. She became a symbol of cynicism and irritation.

Where Are They Now: The Human Memes

The human subjects of memes have taken different paths since their internet fame. Some have embraced their meme status, while others have chosen to stay out of the spotlight.

Bad Luck Brian Today

Kyle Craven, the face behind “Bad Luck Brian,” has capitalized on his meme fame. He has appeared in various commercials and continues to engage with fans on social media.

Success Kid’s Current Status

Sam Griner, the “Success Kid,” has grown up since his meme went viral. His family used his internet fame to raise funds for his father’s kidney transplant, showcasing the positive power of meme fame.

Where Are They Now: The Animal Memes

Animal memes hold a special place in the hearts of internet users. The pets behind these memes often gain massive followings and can even become lucrative brands.

Grumpy Cat’s Legacy

Tardar Sauce, aka Grumpy Cat, passed away in 2019, but her legacy lives on. She amassed a fortune through merchandise, book deals, and appearances, becoming one of the most successful meme brands.

Unexpected Meme Stardom

Sometimes, inanimate objects or unexpected subjects become memes. These instances often highlight the unpredictable nature of internet fame.

The Dress That Divided the Internet

“The Dress” became a viral sensation when viewers disagreed over whether it was blue and black or white and gold. The meme sparked debates and scientific discussions about color perception.

Monetizing Meme Fame

Some meme subjects have found ways to monetize their unexpected fame. This can involve creating merchandise, participating in advertising campaigns, or even starting their own businesses.

Merchandising Memes

Merchandise is a common way for memes to generate revenue. T-shirts, mugs, and other products featuring meme images can be popular items.

Memes in Advertising

Companies often use popular memes in their advertising campaigns to tap into the cultural zeitgeist and appeal to younger audiences.

The Impact of Meme Fame on Personal Lives

While meme fame can be exciting, it also has the potential to impact personal lives. Some individuals experience positive changes, while others may face challenges or unwanted attention.

Positive Outcomes of Meme Fame

For some, meme fame has led to new opportunities, such as media appearances, collaborations, and even charitable fundraising.

The Downsides of Meme Fame

However, not all aspects of meme fame are positive. Some individuals may struggle with privacy issues, bullying, or the pressure to maintain their internet persona.

Preserving Meme History

As memes become an important part of internet culture, efforts are being made to preserve meme history. This includes archiving memes and documenting their origins and impact.

Meme Archives and Exhibitions

Institutions like the Museum of the Moving Image in New York have hosted exhibitions dedicated to memes, recognizing their cultural significance.

Memes as a Reflection of Society

Memes are more than just funny images; they often reflect societal trends, political climates, and generational attitudes. They can serve as a barometer for public sentiment.

Memes and Social Commentary

Many memes provide commentary on current events or social issues, using humor to convey messages and spark discussions.

FAQ Section

  • What is a meme? A meme is a piece of media, typically humorous in nature, that spreads rapidly online, often with slight variations.
  • Can meme fame be monetized? Yes, individuals and brands have monetized meme fame through merchandise, advertising, and media appearances.
  • How do memes impact the people in them? The impact varies; some enjoy the fame and opportunities, while others may face privacy issues or unwanted attention.

Conclusion

The “Meme Where Are They Now?” phenomenon provides a fascinating look into the lives of those who have become internet sensations. While some have leveraged their fame for personal gain or charitable causes, others have retreated from the spotlight, preferring to live a life away from the meme’s shadow. Memes are not just fleeting moments of internet humor; they are cultural artifacts that can have lasting effects on those they feature and the society that shares them. As we continue to create and share memes, it’s worth considering the stories behind the faces and the legacy they leave in the digital world.
