Early Life And Education

Navarone Anthony Garibaldi, the son of actress Priscilla Presley and Marco Garibaldi, was born on March 1, 1987, in the vibrant city of Santa Monica, California. 

Growing up as the half-brother of Lisa Marie Presley, famously known as Elvis Presley’s only child, Navarone’s life was undoubtedly intertwined with Hollywood glamour from the beginning. 

Eventually, as he embarked on his educational journey, he enrolled in a prestigious high school in Los Angeles.

Career

Garibaldi established the rock band Them Guns in 2009 alongside guitarist Reese Rambach, bassist Kyle Cunningham, and drummer Kody Foster. Their performances at renowned Los Angeles venues such as The Viper Room and The Troubadour swiftly garnered them a dedicated fanbase. 

Four years later, in 2013, Them Guns received acclaim for their debut EP, “The Dirty Work,” which was highly praised for its infectious hooks and unfiltered energy.

The Guns have recently been touring and releasing new music. Their latest EP, “Live Fast, Die Beautiful,” was released in 2016. Garibaldi, the band’s frontman, has also been working on solo projects and dropped the single “Bad Boy” in 2018. 

Known for his strong vocals, captivating stage presence, and emotional connection with audiences, Garibaldi is becoming a prominent figure in rock music. His music will certainly continue to develop and flourish in the future.

After releasing their second full-length album, “Electric on the Attack,” in 2015, Them Guns solidified their prominent presence in the rock scene. 

The album’s weightier sound and more profound themes deeply connected with their fans, propelling the band to embark on numerous extensive tours. Their electrifying stage presence and mesmerizing melodies garnered them widespread acclaim and recognition.
