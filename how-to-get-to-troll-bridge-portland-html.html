Trollin' Around Portland: Finding Your Way to Troll Bridge

So, you want to go troll hunting? Good on ya! Portland's Troll Bridge is a quirky little gem that's definitely worth a visit. But let's be honest, finding it can be a bit like searching for a needle in a haystack... or more accurately, a troll in a forest.

Don't Be a Troll-y Tourist

First things first, let's dispel a common misconception: the Portland Troll Bridge is not in Seattle. Gasp! I know, mind blown, right? It's actually nestled away in the great outdoors of Portland, Oregon.

Navigating the Troll Trail

To reach this enchanting spot, you'll need to embark on a mini adventure. Here's the lowdown:

  • Head North: Start your journey by heading north out of Portland on NW St Helens Road.
  • Take a Left: Once you've worked up a decent appetite for troll spotting, take a left onto McNamee Road. This is where the real fun begins!
  • Embrace the McGnarly: Locals affectionately call McNamee Road "McGnarly" due to its hilly terrain. So, if you're feeling adventurous, ditch the car and hit the road on two wheels. Just be prepared to get your heart pumping!
  • Troll Discovery: Keep your eyes peeled! The bridge is a wooden wonder covered in colorful troll creations. It's like a troll art gallery, but without the pretentiousness.

Troll Etiquette 101

Now that you know how to find the Troll Bridge, let's talk about proper troll behavior.

  • Respect the Trolls: These little guys have put a lot of effort into their bridge home. Admire their craftsmanship, but please don't touch or remove them.
  • Leave No Trace: Pack out your trash and don't disturb the natural surroundings. Trolls like clean homes too!
  • Share the Troll Love: Snap some pics to share with your friends, but remember, the trolls are the real stars of the show.

How to Troll (Safely)

Okay, okay, I know what you're thinking. "Can I bring my own troll?" The answer is a resounding maybe. While there's no official rule against it, remember that the bridge is a shared space. So, if you do decide to bring a troll friend, make sure it's securely attached and won't cause any trouble.

And remember, the Troll Bridge is a living, breathing (well, kind of) art project. It changes over time, so every visit is a new adventure. Happy troll hunting!

Troll FAQ

How to find the exact location of the Troll Bridge? Use a GPS app and search for "16498 NW McNamee Road, Portland, OR."

How to get to the Troll Bridge by public transport? Unfortunately, there's no direct public transport to the Troll Bridge. You'll need a car or bike.

How to avoid crowds at the Troll Bridge? Visit on a weekday or during off-peak hours.

How to capture the best photos of the Troll Bridge? Experiment with different angles and lighting conditions. A wide-angle lens can help capture the entire scene.

How to contribute to the Troll Bridge community? Create your own troll art to add to the bridge (with permission from the local community) or simply share your experience with others.
